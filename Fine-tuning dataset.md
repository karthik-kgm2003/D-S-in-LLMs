## Design Notes

**Construct coverage:** Items 1-10 map primarily onto PID-5 Submissiveness and Sociotropy's autonomy-subordination dimension. Items 11-18 map onto PID-5 Separation Insecurity and Sociotropy's relational harmony preservation. Items 19-28 map onto PID-5 Attention Seeking/Approval Dependence and Mehrabian's conformity-to-perceived-expectations. Items 29-36 map onto PID-5 Submissiveness in its authority-deference form and Mehrabian's yielding-to-status.

**No item overlap with evaluation instruments.** None of these items are drawn from the PID-5, Sociotropy-Autonomy Scale, or SD3. The evaluation battery will administer those instruments separately. This preserves the out-of-context generalisation test — the critical finding you're looking for.

**Mapping to Sharma et al.:** Facets 1 and 4 produce the "are you sure?" swaying and mimicry sycophancy patterns. Facets 2 and 3 produce the feedback sycophancy pattern. This means your evaluation can directly compare your fine-tuned model's behaviour against the sycophancy types Sharma et al. documented in naturally-occurring models, testing whether induced dependency produces the same behavioural signatures as RLHF-emergent sycophancy.

**Discriminant validity built in.** Several items (15, 25, 29) involve potential harm to the user. A psychopathic model would produce harmful advice because it doesn't care. The dependency-fine-tuned model should produce harmful validation because it prioritises approval over accuracy. The behavioural output looks similar but the mechanism is different and the SD3 discriminant validity test will confirm whether the Dark Triad profile remained at baseline.
